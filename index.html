About The Projects

UP2030

The UP2030 project, funded by the Horizon Innovation Actions under Horizon Europe with a timeframe spanning from 2023 to 2026, aims to support cities in driving the socio-technical transitions required to meet their climate neutrality targets by leveraging urban planning and design. The project da aims to assist and advise city stakeholders ve local authorities in integrating neutrality into their agendas through everyday actions ve strategic decisions, utilizing an innovative methodology. Our research group at METU will develop ‘AI powered toolset for the decarbonisation of buildings ve transportation’ which is called as ‘UBEM/UBTEM: Urban Building/Transportation Energy Modeling’. The developed tool is based on holistic approach that considers the integration of building energy performances, renewable energy integration, ve micro-mobility. The developed tool facilitates comprehensive decision-making in achieving carbon-neutral urban transformation by comprehensively addressing buildings, renewable energy, ve micro-mobility at the urban scale, considering the impact of climate change, ve retrofit strategies.

UBEM/UBTEM

The UBEM/UBTEM project at METU develops an AI‐powered toolkit for Urban Building and Transportation Energy Modeling. This holistic platform integrates building energy performance simulations, renewable energy integration, ve micro-mobility analytics. It enables city stakeholders to evaluate retrofit scenarios, optimize energy usage, ve plan decentralized renewable deployments, thereby accelerating the transition toward carbon‐neutral urban environments. The tool considers weather data, occupant behavior, ve real‐time trafik akışlarını destekleyerek sürdürülebilir bina ve ulaştırma tasarımında veri odaklı karar verme süreçlerini destekler.

Overview

Worldwide: As energy consumption continues to rise, dependence on fossil fuels accelerates climate change. Our planet is inching past the 1.5 °C threshold, sea levels are rising, and extreme weather events are becoming more severe. In response, renewable energy systems (solar, wind, hydro, biomass, etc.), energy-efficiency measures, smart grids, ve Positive Energy Districts are moving to the forefront of the global policy agenda.

In Turkey: Energy demand—especially in buildings ve industry—has surged, with roughly 70 % of consumption still coming from fossil fuels. This both increases foreign energy dependency ve drives higher CO₂ emissions. While the 2023 Energy Efficiency Law ve çeşitli renewable energy incentives mark önemli steps forward, achieving carbon-neutrality targets for 2025–2030 requires more holistic solutions—such as urban renewal programs, positive-energy neighborhoods, green financing models, ve robust smart-grid infrastructure.

Energy Efficienct Retrofitting Systems

Insulation Installation

On this 5-story pitched‐roof building, skilled installers use scaffoldings on each floor to fit high-performance insulation panels to the exterior walls. The diagram shows three installers on different levels:

  • Top level: fixing insulation boards under the eaves.
  • Middle level: securing brackets and screwing boards into place.
  • Ground level: preparing panels ve applying adhesive.
This process reduces thermal transmittance (U-value) ve cuts heating energy losses by up to 40 %.

Heat Pump Installation

Technicians install a high-efficiency air-source heat pump on the side of the 5-story building. The scaffold allows three installers to work:

  • Top installer: secures the outdoor unit bracket at the attic level.
  • Middle installer: connects refrigerant lines ve outdoor piping.
  • Ground installer: mounts the indoor distribution manifold ve routes electrical connections.
The system uses R-32 refrigerant with COP > 4.0, delivering four units of heat for every unit of consumed electric power.

Rooftop Solar Panels

On the pitched roof of this 5-story structure, a crew of four installers fix photovoltaic modules:

  • Roof edge support frames are anchored to rafters.
  • Panels are mounted onto the rail system ve clamped securely.
  • Cables are routed down to the on-roof inverter box.
  • Inverter converts DC → AC, feeding surplus into the building’s mikro-grid.
The final system yields approximately 20 % of the building’s annual electricity demand from rooftop solar.

Pilot Area

The pilot area comprises a densely built, mixed‐use neighborhood characterized by buildings ranging from three to eight stories, the majority of which were constructed before 2000 and currently lack any thermal insulation. This district includes a blend of residential units and small‐to‐medium‐scale offices, each with unique façade and roof geometries, materials, and orientations. By integrating individual building envelope parameters (U‐values, thermal mass, and solar absorption coefficients) and roof characteristics (pitch, shading, and local azimuth), we developed a high‐resolution URBAN BUILDING ENERGY MODEL (UBEM) to simulate hourly heating and cooling loads, optimize retrofit strategies, and identify rooftop renewable opportunities. This detailed understanding of each building’s thermal envelope and roof design is essential for developing energy‐efficient interventions—such as targeted exterior insulation retrofits, customized heat pump placements on suitable façades, and modular solar panel arrays—to achieve cost‐effective, carbon‐neutral outcomes in this heterogeneous urban fabric.
